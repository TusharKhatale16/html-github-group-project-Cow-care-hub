Veterinary assistance

Veterinary assistance is important for maintaining the health and safety of cows. It provides guidance on common treatments such as vaccination, deworming, and basic injury care. This service helps cow owners understand when it is necessary to contact a veterinarian, especially in cases of fever, loss of appetite, injury, or unusual behavior. Timely veterinary care helps prevent serious health problems and ensures proper treatment.

Health check guidance

Health check guidance focuses on regular monitoring of a cow’s physical condition and behavior. By observing eating habits, movement, milk production, and general activity, cow owners can identify early signs of illness. Regular health checks help in early detection of problems and reduce the chances of severe diseases. This practice supports long-term cow health and well-being.

Nutrition support

Nutrition support provides information on proper feeding practices based on the cow’s age, breed, and purpose. A balanced diet that includes green fodder, dry fodder, clean water, and essential nutrients is necessary for good health. Proper nutrition helps improve immunity, growth, and milk production, ensuring better productivity and care.

Disease awareness

Disease awareness helps cow owners understand common diseases and recognize early symptoms such as fever, weakness, diarrhea, or reduced milk yield. This service promotes preventive measures like vaccination, cleanliness, and timely treatment. Early disease awareness helps protect cows from serious illness and reduces health-related losses.
